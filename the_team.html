Our Team

Michael Lo

Founder and President

Michael Lo started AAPI Heroes after asking a simple question: Who were the historical heroes of the AAPI community, and how could he share their stories with his digital-first generation? Michael shepherded the site’s development, from his initial vision to the final product. He oversees site research, design, content, in addition to personnel management, recruitment, and leadership diversity.



Pranav Eranki

Head Developer and Debugger

Pranav was the head developer and debugger for the website, developing numerous iterations of the website for scalability and compatibility among different devices, as well as improving the interface. He worked closely with Michael and the designers to optimize the website. He is currently a senior at Cupertino High School.



Eric Hu

Assistant Debugger and Developer

Eric Hu worked along with Pranav Eranki and Michael to develop the website, helping both contribute to the initial code and debug the final product. He joined the team to raise awareness for the efforts of Asian Americans and Pacific-Islanders during the Civil War. He is currently a senior at Cupertino High.



Kathleen Hua

Graphic Designer

Kathleen is the Graphic Designer of Historical Stories Interactive. She composes the backgrounds, buttons, and dog tags for each page. She is a current junior at Cupertino High School.

Bellemy Morgan

Logo Designer

Bellemy created the logo and favicon of the website. She enjoys creating for the purpose of education and was inspired by AAPI Heroes’ mission. She is a current junior at Cupertino High School.

Zach Uriarte

Head Researcher

Zach curated the personal stories of the heroes highlighted in the website, along with adding depth to the historical context provided. He joined the team due to both his passion for history, and his desire to recognize the considerable impacts of Asian-American immigrants in United States history. He is currently a senior attending Lynbrook High School.



Alexander Wang

Head of Content Development

Alexander Wang is responsible for managing and overseeing content development and research for the website. As the first partner brought on, he played a pivotal role in the site's initial development and continues to manage and curate the stories presented. He is a current senior at Cupertino High.
